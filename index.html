About

Why "Tessera"?

tessera
tes·ser·a  ·  TESS-er-uh  ·  noun  ·  plural tesserae
A small block of stone, tile, glass, or other material used in the making of a mosaic.

On its own, a single tile doesn't add up to much — its meaning comes from being set alongside many others. That's the idea behind the name: every volunteer, group, task, material run, and project is one tessera, a single piece of the event. No one piece finishes the work; the whole event only comes together once each is set in its place.

So that's what Tessera is for: helping place each piece where it belongs, so the larger mission comes together. You can see it in our mark — a home built from mosaic tiles, with one tile tilted, just being set into place.

Install

Add Tessera to your phone

Tessera is a Progressive Web App — no app store required. Follow the steps for your phone to add it to your home screen like any other app.

🍎

iPhone & iPad

Safari browser required

  1. 1

    Open app.tesseramissions.org in Safari, then tap the ••• button in the bottom-right corner.

  2. 2

    Tap Share.

  3. 3

    Tap View More.

  4. 4

    Tap Add to Home Screen.

  5. 5

    Tap Add. Tessera will appear on your home screen.

🤖

Android

Chrome browser required

  1. 1

    Open Chrome and go to app.tesseramissions.org

  2. 2

    Tap the three dots (⋮) in the top-right corner

  3. 3

    Tap "Add to Home Screen" or "Install App"

  4. 4

    Tap "Add" to confirm. Tessera will appear on your home screen.

Role guides

Find your role

Every person at a Tessera event has a role. Select yours to see what you'll do, which pages you'll use, and what to do first when you arrive.

→

Group Leader

Leads a team of volunteers at a worksite. Coordinates with the Site Coach and requests materials as the project progresses.
→

Site Coach

Oversees project quality and scope. Supports group leaders, logs project progress, and connects sites to event leadership.
→

Material Runner

Delivers supplies and tools to worksites. Fulfills material requests, coordinates with the warehouse, and logs deliveries.
→

Event Coordinator

Oversees the entire event. Monitors all sites, manages logistics, handles escalations, and keeps everything moving.
→

Hotshot

A skilled float team member dispatched to worksites that need extra help. Handles specialized tasks or urgent project needs.
→

Support

Handles backend logistics — shopping, warehouse, check-in, food, and other coordination that keeps the event running.

Not sure what role you have? Check the FAQ or ask your group leader.

FAQ

Common questions

Answers to the most frequent questions volunteers have before and during an event.

See the install guide above for step-by-step instructions for iPhone and Android. On iPhone, you must use Safari (not Chrome). On Android, use Chrome. The app does not require an app store download.
Notifications may be blocked if you tapped "Don't Allow" when first prompted. To fix it: on iPhone, go to Settings → Notifications → Tessera and turn on Allow Notifications. On Android, go to Settings → Apps → Tessera → Notifications. Make sure your phone is not on Do Not Disturb during the event.
On the login screen, tap "Forgot PIN?" and follow the reset steps. If you're at the event and can't get in, find your group leader or an event coordinator — they can look up your access.
Inside the app, navigate to your worksite's Photos tab. Tap the camera icon to take a photo directly, or the gallery icon to upload from your camera roll. Label the photo as "Before" or "After" when prompted. Photos sync automatically when you have a signal.
Go to your project page and tap "Request Materials." Describe what you need and how many — a Material Runner will see the request and deliver to your site. For urgent needs, contact your Site Coach directly.
First, check your phone's signal or Wi-Fi connection. If you have a connection and the app still won't load, close it completely and reopen it. If that doesn't help, try uninstalling and reinstalling from app.tesseramissions.org. Find a coordinator if the problem continues on event day.
Your role is assigned by event leadership before the event. You may have been told in an email, at a pre-event meeting, or by your group leader. If you're unsure, ask your group leader or an event coordinator. Most volunteers will be in a group — if that's you, read the Group Leader guide or just follow your group leader's instructions.
